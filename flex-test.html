GMAT Prep Tips

Seperate fact from fiction when it comes to prepping for the GMAT, and don't fall for these common test-taking myths!

Malikah Coleman
Business School,
Northwood University
8 a.m. Wake up, eat breakfast, and get ready to study.
9 a.m. Hit the library, like last night, to study the article that will be discussed in class today. Most classes are student-facilitated with the instructor present for questions and additional guidance.
10:30 a.m. Meet my small group to finish our project based on a real-life case study. Coursework usually teaches business principles through case studies, and group projects focus on how to apply them.
Noon Go to the cafeteria to grab a quick snack before heading to class.
1 p.m. My only class of the day. It has about 30 people, and today my group is guiding the discussion. We open with a Q&A, then work through the article.
3 p.m. Class is over. Time to work independently on another project I have coming up.
5 p.m. Let my brain breathe a bit. Tonight it’s dinner with friends. I look forward to these daily breaks—they help me stay motivated throughout the day.
8 p.m. Back to hitting the books. Study the article for class tomorrow or another upcoming class.
10 p.m. Wind down, prepare for the next day, and turn in.
